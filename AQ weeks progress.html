Five-Week Progress of a Community Service Project on STUDY ON THE PILIMINARY KNOWLEDGE OF AQUACULTURE

ACTIVITY GALLERY

The community service project undertaken in this abstract focuses on aquafarming, specifically in the context of freshwater, brackish water, and saltwater populations. Aquafarming involves the controlled cultivation of aquatic organisms, such as fish, in artificial facilities like fish tanks, ponds, and raceways. The project aims to create general awareness in society about aquafarming and its benefits.

In the initial week, the project coordinator met with a VRO (Village Revenue Officer) officer to explain the community service project assigned by JNTUA (Jawaharlal Nehru Technological University Anantapur). The chosen topic for the project is aquafarming, and to conduct it effectively, the project coordinator verified several seafood areas in the local vicinity.

Aquafarming is recognized as an efficient and sustainable method to produce high-quality protein. It has a low carbon footprint and feed conversion ratio, while retaining high levels of protein and energy. Skretting, a company mentioned in the context, provides innovative and sustainable nutritional solutions to support farmed fish and shrimp.

During a visit to the fish market, the project coordinator observed the bustling and crowded environment with various types of fish being sold by vendors. They interacted with a fisherman who shared insights about fishing gear and the types of fish found in rivers. The data for the project were collected with the help of local fishermen, fish farmers, and vendors.

Week 3 of the project involved exploring topics like the optimal size and shape of ponds for aquaculture, power supply considerations, soil types and fertility, and the management of aquatic weeds. The importance of a well-maintained hatchery for shrimp seed production and the elimination of unwanted fishes from ponds were also discussed.

The abstract briefly mentions two common diseases affecting fish: "red pest" and anchor worms. The symptoms and treatment options for these diseases are outlined. The three main types of fish commonly harvested by farmers in the area are mentioned: GANDI, BOCHA, and ROOPCHAND.

The abstract highlights the significance of manufactured feeds in modern aquaculture, providing balanced nutrition for farmed fish. Various types of feeds, including fish meal, soybean meal, and maize gluten, are mentioned. Additionally, the use of aqua chemicals for maintaining industrial cooling systems and the importance of oxygen levels, proper feeding practices, and sanitization in fish farming are discussed.

Transportation of fish for export is mentioned, and the abstract concludes with an overview of activities in the fourth and fifth weeks of the project. These activities involve identifying problems related to fish maintenance and diseases, researching and implementing treatment solutions, and monitoring the success of the treatments.

Overall, this abstract provides an overview of a community service project focused on aquafarming, including its benefits, challenges, and the efforts made to address them through research, data collection, and treatment implementation.

Conclusion

In conclusion, this community service project on aquafarming aims to raise awareness about the benefits and challenges of cultivating aquatic organisms. Through research, data collection, and treatment implementation, the project seeks to promote sustainable aquaculture practices. By addressing issues such as disease management, feed nutrition, and transportation, the project aims to contribute to the success and efficiency of aquafarming operations.
